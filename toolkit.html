Oscillators — the raw tone

Where sound begins. Build a tone by stacking sine harmonics (additive), or by letting one oscillator bend another's pitch (FM). Both are band-limited, so they never alias.

Additive workbench — drawbars

Pull each harmonic's amplitude. The waveform forms as you go; above Nyquist, harmonics grey out and drop.

FM workbench — two operators

A modulator bends the carrier's frequency. Whole-number ratios stay harmonic; odd ratios go metallic/bell-like. Raise the index for more sidebands.

Filters — carving the tone

Subtractive synthesis: start rich, then remove. A resonant lowpass is the soul of analog. Sweep the cutoff while it plays — that gesture is half of electronic music.

Filter workbench

Pick a waveform, choose a filter type, and sweep. Resonance emphasizes the cutoff frequency.

live output
Try a saw through a lowpass with resonance ~12, and drag the cutoff slowly. That's the classic sweep.

Envelopes & modulation — shape & motion

An envelope shapes one event over time (attack→decay→sustain→release). An LFO is a slow oscillator that moves a parameter — vibrato, tremolo, a filter that breathes. Motion is what makes a sound alive.

Envelope & LFO workbench

Set the ADSR (it shapes the volume), then add an LFO and choose what it modulates. Press Trigger to hear one note.
