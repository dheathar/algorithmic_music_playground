The dream

Every track needs a source. This one grows from a childhood dream — translated, element by element, into sound. You don't need to be a musician for this; you need a feeling and a way to map it.

A black theater, the audience at my back. I am a child alone on the dark stage, fishing into the black backdrop — abstract and lonely, in a big world full of eyes.

The dream, as a score

CARPET

Audience → drone

The world full of eyes. An open-fifth drone — no third, so "black", undecided — breathing slowly. Everything rests on it.

VOICE

Child → wind

You: "an organ like a wind." A breathy, trembling flute voice, small and exposed above the dark.

GESTURE

Fishing → soundings

The experience that awaits. Deep soundings cast into the void, ringing out — lonely, probing, unanswered.

The arc — the eyes over a life

The eyes are not one thing. They can be kind, adoring, envious — or they can close. The piece moves through three movements as the gaze changes; the drone itself carries the story.

I0:00 – 0:30

Kind eyes

Childhood. Most eyes are warm and nice. A gentle third softens the black; the room is small and safe; the child sings simply.

II0:30 – 1:06

Adoration & envy

Getting better. The crowd swells — a bright adoration shimmer and, beneath it, a dissonant envy cluster (Bb grinding on A). Grand, dazzling, unstable. The child soars and strains.

III1:06 – 1:36

The eyes close

The end is near. Voices fall away, the filter darkens (turning away), the room empties to silence. One last sounding into the void — unanswered.

Volume & dimensions

"Organs" are the voices of the piece. Each is given a volume and a place in a three-dimensional field: left↔right (stereo), high↔low (register), near↔far (reverb & level). You are at the bottom centre, looking into the dark.

↑ high · far↓ low · near leftright
Glint the line · top
Audience eyes · wide L
Audience eyes · wide R
Fishing roaming · far →
Child centre · near
Pulse heartbeat · time
Pedal stage floor · bottom
YOU · the child on the stage

Mix volumes: audience 0.90 · child 0.70 · fishing 0.60 · pedal 0.50 · pulse 0.40 · glint 0.30. The stereo width itself is automated — widest in movement II, collapsing to mono as the eyes close (measured side-energy 0.071 → 0.126 → 0.048).

Icarus, or the Effort of Flight

The project's second creation — a flying dream fused with cosmic horror, built on a real recording of Jupiter. You can fly, but only by clapping furiously; you rise above the safe rooftops into joy, and keep rising into the vast, indifferent churn of the cosmos. The higher the joy, the closer the horror.

I dreamt I could fly — but I had to clap my hands really fast. I flew just above the rooftops. The feeling was great… but the effort to keep the altitude was hard.

The seeds, as organs

EFFORT

Clapping → the Clap

A fast motoric pulse (~8/sec). It is the effort; when it thins, you sag and fall.

JOY + STRAIN

Flight → Altitude

A soaring Lydian pad & lead — the brightest mode, the "flight" mode. It sags and recovers with the effort.

HOME

Rooftops → warmth

A warm low chord below: safety, the human world, receding as you climb.

THE COSMOS

Jupiter → the Void

The ion-acoustic recording, engineered into the abyss — looming louder the higher you go.

WRONGNESS

Horror → daemon flutes

Alien whole-tone piping that appears at altitude — the cosmos is not empty.

TENSION

Lydian vs whole-tone

Human joy (bright, tonal) against the cosmos (floating, no home). The clash is the feeling.

The flight — four stages

I0:00–0:35

Takeoff

Warm rooftops, the clap begins, you lift into the first joy. The void is only a rumor.

II0:35–1:30

Climb

The clap drives hard, altitude rises and strains, the Jupiter churn creeps in from above.

III1:30–2:15

The height

High up: the void roars, daemon flutes pipe, beauty and terror at once — maximum effort.

IV2:15–3:00

Descent

The clap tires, altitude sags, you sink back toward the rooftops — relief and loss, unresolved.

Method

Two tools, one handshake. Python invents the sounds; GarageBand arranges and mixes them. They line up only if both agree on a few numbers.

PYTHON ENGINE

Sound-design lab

Unique oscillators, filters, phaser, drift, stereo space — written from scratch. Renders one WAV per organ.

— stems →
GARAGEBAND

Arrange & mix

Each stem on its own track: volume, pan, effects, mute / solo. Visualise the waveforms and polish.
